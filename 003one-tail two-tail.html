The Difference Between One-Tail and Two-Tail Hypothesis Tests

One-Tail Test (Unidirectional) ➡️

A one-tail (or **one-sided**) test is used when the alternative hypothesis specifies a **direction** for the effect or difference.

  • Alternative Hypothesis ($H_A$): Claims the parameter is *greater than* **(>)** or *less than* **(<)** the null value.
  • Critical Region: The rejection region is located entirely in **one tail** (either the far left or the far right) of the distribution.
  • When to Use: When the research question asks if something has *increased* or *decreased*. (e.g., "Is the new average score higher than 75?")

Two-Tail Test (Non-directional) ↕️

A two-tail (or **two-sided**) test is used when the alternative hypothesis does **not** specify a direction, only that a difference exists.

  • Alternative Hypothesis ($H_A$): Claims the parameter is simply *not equal to* **($\neq$)** the null value.
  • Critical Region: The rejection region is split equally between **both tails** of the distribution.
  • When to Use: When the research question asks only if something is *different* from a given value. (e.g., "Is the new average score different from 75?")
