Statistical Analysis Scenarios with Calculations

Below are ten scenarios requiring a one-sample z-test or t-test. For each, the first three steps are provided, followed by the test statistic calculation, a p-value calculation using Excel’s method, and a question to determine the true p-value. Finally, interpret the results (assume α = 0.05). The parameter tested is μ (population mean).

Scenario 1: Factory Machine Output

A factory claims its machines produce widgets with an average weight of 50 grams (μ). The population standard deviation is 2 grams. A sample of 40 widgets has a mean weight of 50.8 grams. Test if the average weight differs from 50 grams.

Step 1: Hypotheses
H₀: μ = 50, H₁: μ ≠ 50

Step 2: Testing Method
One-sample z-test (population standard deviation known)

Step 3: Test Type
Two-tail test

Step 4: Test Statistic
Given: x̄ = 50.8, μ = 50, σ = 2, n = 40
Test statistic: z = (x̄ - μ) / (σ / √n) = (50.8 - 50) / (2 / √40) ≈ 2.53

Step 5: P-value
Excel calculation: NORM.S.DIST(2.53, TRUE) ≈ 0.9943
Question: Is the true p-value p, 1-p, 2p, or 2*(1-p)? Calculate the true p-value.

Step 6: Interpret Results
Assuming α = 0.05, interpret whether to reject H₀ and what this means about the widget weights.

Scenario 2: Student Test Scores

A school claims its students score an average of 75 points (μ) on a standardized test, with population variance unknown. A sample of 25 students has a mean score of 78 points and a sample standard deviation of 5 points. Test if the average score is higher than 75 points.

Step 1: Hypotheses
H₀: μ = 75, H₁: μ > 75

Step 2: Testing Method
One-sample t-test (population variance unknown)

Step 3: Test Type
One-tail test (right-tailed)

Step 4: Test Statistic
Given: x̄ = 78, μ = 75, s = 5, n = 25
Test statistic: t = (x̄ - μ) / (s / √n) = (78 - 75) / (5 / √25) = 3.00

Step 5: P-value
Excel calculation (approximate, using t-distribution): T.DIST.RT(3.00, 24) ≈ 0.0031
Question: Is the true p-value p, 1-p, 2p, or 2*(1-p)? Calculate the true p-value.

Step 6: Interpret Results
Assuming α = 0.05, interpret whether to reject H₀ and what this means about the test scores.
