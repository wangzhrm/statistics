Factors Influencing Parenthood Decisions

A data-driven look at reasons young people choose not to have children

The Problem

Many modern societies are experiencing declining birth rates. This analysis aims to explore the key factors influencing young people's decisions to have or not have children. By examining a simulated dataset on various reasons and their perceived importance, we can identify major trends and specific differences between genders, particularly regarding economic concerns.

Dataset

This dataset represents a simulated survey of 50 young adults. The data includes the perceived importance of different reasons for not having children, rated on a scale of 1 (Not Important at All) to 5 (Extremely Important).
