Population vs Sample Statistics Activity

Understanding Parameters and Statistics through Data Analysis

Activity Overview

In this activity, we will explore the differences between population parameters and sample statistics by analyzing distance data from our class.

Activity Steps

1
Each student fills out the online form that includes variables including name, gender, and distance from home to UESTC campus.
2
One student randomly selects several students from the class roster.
3
Another student selects several students sitting in the back of the classroom.
4
Calculate the population mean and standard deviation for all students.
5
Calculate the sample mean and standard deviation for the randomly selected students.
6
Calculate the sample mean and standard deviation for the students from the back of the classroom.
7
Compare the population parameters with the sample statistics.
8
Discuss why differences exist between parameters and statistics.
9
Analyze why the back-of-class sample might differ from the random sample.

Discussion Questions

1. What differences do you observe between the population parameters and sample statistics?

2. Why are there differences between the population mean and the sample means?

3. How does the random sample compare to the back-of-class sample? Why might they differ?

4. What might be the reasons for the difference between the back sample and the population?

5. Is the back-of-class sample representative of the population? Why or why not?

6. What are the implications of these differences for statistical inference?

7. How does sample size affect the accuracy of statistics?

8. Why is random sampling important in statistics?
