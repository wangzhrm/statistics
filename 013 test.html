Statistics Test Paper

1. A researcher wants to test if the average weight of a new brand of cereal boxes is greater than 500 grams. The population variance is known to be 16 grams², and a sample of 40 boxes is taken. State the null and alternative hypotheses, the appropriate statistical test, and whether it is a one-tail or two-tail test.

2. A teacher claims that the average score on a math test is different from 75. A sample of 25 students is taken, and the population variance is unknown. State the null and alternative hypotheses, the appropriate statistical test, and whether it is a one-tail or two-tail test.

3. A researcher compares the average heights of two groups of students (30 in each group). An F-test shows equal variances, and the population variances are unknown. The researcher wants to test if the mean heights differ. State the null and alternative hypotheses, the appropriate statistical test, and whether it is a one-tail or two-tail test.

4. A company tests if a new machine produces parts faster than an old machine. Two independent samples (20 parts from each) are taken, and an F-test shows unequal variances. The population variances are unknown. State the null and alternative hypotheses, the appropriate statistical test, and whether it is a one-tail or two-tail test.

5. A researcher tests if a sample of 20 items has a mean different from 100. The population variance is unknown. Excel output for a one-sample t-test (two-tailed, α = 0.05) shows:

t Stat2.98
df19
p-value (two-tail)0.003

Interpret the results from the Excel output.

6. A quality control manager tests if the average defect rate is less than 50 defects per batch. The population variance is known, and a sample of 35 batches is taken. Excel output for a one-sample z-test (left-tailed, α = 0.05) shows:

z Stat-1.41
p-value (one-tail)0.08

Interpret the results from the Excel output.

7. A researcher compares the effectiveness of two teaching methods using two groups (25 students each). An F-test confirms equal variances. Excel output for a two-sample t-test (equal variances, two-tailed, α = 0.05) shows:

t Stat2.10
df48
p-value (two-tail)0.04

Interpret the results from the Excel output.

8. A company tests if a new production method yields higher output than the old method. Two independent samples (15 units each) are taken, and an F-test shows unequal variances. Excel output for a two-sample t-test (unequal variances, right-tailed, α = 0.05) shows:

t Stat2.20
df27
p-value (one-tail)0.02

Interpret the results from the Excel output.
