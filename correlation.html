Ice Cream Sales & Drowning Incidents

A Case Study in Correlation vs Causation

The Story

In the coastal town of Sunnyville, researchers noticed something alarming. During the summer months, both ice cream sales and drowning incidents were increasing at an alarming rate.

The town council collected data over the past five years and found a strong correlation (r = 0.92) between ice cream sales and drowning incidents. The mayor presented these findings, suggesting that ice cream consumption might be causing people to drown.

"The data clearly shows," the mayor stated, "that as ice cream sales increase, so do drowning deaths. We must consider regulating ice cream sales to protect our citizens!"

Class Activity: What's Wrong With This Analysis?

Discuss with your group why the town's conclusion might be incorrect. Consider what other factors could explain this correlation.
