Statistical Analysis Scenarios - Complete

Below are ten scenarios with all six steps of statistical analysis completed for a one-sample z-test or t-test. The parameter tested is μ (population mean). The significance level is α = 0.05.

Scenario 1: Factory Machine Output

A factory claims its machines produce widgets with an average weight of 50 grams (μ). The population standard deviation is 2 grams. A sample of 40 widgets has a mean weight of 50.8 grams. Test if the average weight differs from 50 grams.

Step 1: Hypotheses
H₀: μ = 50, H₁: μ ≠ 50

Step 2: Testing Method
One-sample z-test (population standard deviation known)

Step 3: Test Type
Two-tail test

Step 4: Test Statistic
Given: x̄ = 50.8, μ = 50, σ = 2, n = 40
Test statistic: z = (x̄ - μ) / (σ / √n) = (50.8 - 50) / (2 / √40) ≈ 2.53

Step 5: P-value
Excel calculation: NORM.S.DIST(2.53, TRUE) ≈ 0.9943
For a two-tail test, true p-value = 2 * (1 - NORM.S.DIST(|z|, TRUE)) = 2 * (1 - 0.9943) = 2 * 0.0057 ≈ 0.0114

Step 6: Interpret Results
Since p = 0.0114 < 0.05, reject H₀. There is sufficient evidence to conclude that the average widget weight differs from 50 grams.

Scenario 2: Student Test Scores

A school claims its students score an average of 75 points (μ) on a standardized test, with population variance unknown. A sample of 25 students has a mean score of 78 points and a sample standard deviation of 5 points. Test if the average score is higher than 75 points.

Step 1: Hypotheses
H₀: μ = 75, H₁: μ > 75

Step 2: Testing Method
One-sample t-test (population variance unknown)

Step 3: Test Type
One-tail test (right-tailed)

Step 4: Test Statistic
Given: x̄ = 78, μ = 75, s = 5, n = 25
Test statistic: t = (x̄ - μ) / (s / √n) = (78 - 75) / (5 / √25) = 3.00

Step 5: P-value
Excel calculation: T.DIST.RT(3.00, 24) ≈ 0.0031
For a right-tail test, true p-value = T.DIST.RT(3.00, 24) = 0.0031

Step 6: Interpret Results
Since p = 0.0031 < 0.05, reject H₀. There is sufficient evidence to conclude that the average test score is higher than 75 points.

Scenario 4: Coffee Shop Wait Time

A coffee shop claims customers wait an average of 5 minutes (μ) for orders, with population variance unknown. A sample of 30 customers has a mean wait time of 5.4 minutes and a sample standard deviation of 1.2 minutes. Test if the average wait time differs from 5 minutes.

Step 1: Hypotheses
H₀: μ = 5, H₁: μ ≠ 5

Step 2: Testing Method
One-sample t-test (population variance unknown)

Step 3: Test Type
Two-tail test

Step 4: Test Statistic
Given: x̄ = 5.4, μ = 5, s = 1.2, n = 30
Test statistic: t = (x̄ - μ) / (s / √n) = (5.4 - 5) / (1.2 / √30) ≈ 1.83

Step 5: P-value
Excel calculation (one tail): T.DIST(1.83, 29, TRUE) ≈ 0.9611
